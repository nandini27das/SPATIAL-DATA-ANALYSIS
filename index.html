About

Floods are a frequent natural disaster that inflict significant losses on the environment, the economy, and the population in India. Understanding the mechanics of floods is crucial for disaster management and preparedness in this diverse and disaster susceptible country. In India, floods rank among the most devastating and most common natural disasters, taking millions of lives each year. These floods can be caused by a variety of factors, such as heavy rains, river overflow, cyclones, snowmelt, and dam failures. Since floods are directly related to India's topography, climate, and monsoon patterns, it is imperative to comprehend their causes, historical context, geographic distribution, and mitigation strategies. Flooding, one of India's deadliest and most common natural disasters, kills millions of people every year. Statistical methods are helpful in analyzing numerical data, but they are not appropriate for characterizing the "methodological form" of floods, which is a complex system affected by several factors.
